Palmetto Business Automation Website Full Development Log

Project Overview
----------------
Business: Palmetto Business Automation, LLC
Public Name: Palmetto Business Automation
Site Type: Static marketing website for a service-area business
Primary Goal: Present the business, explain services, show example builds, route visitors to Microsoft Bookings or text contact, and support future growth with a professional portfolio-style presence.

Initial Business / Website Direction
------------------------------------
The site was planned as a low-cost, simple, professional website for a home-based service business.

Core business constraints and preferences:
- Do not show the home address publicly
- Use service-area business framing
- Support in-person or virtual meetings
- Keep costs low
- Do not require Google Workspace
- Keep Microsoft 365 for business operations later
- Use a dedicated Google account for business setup
- Use Microsoft Bookings for discovery calls
- Use a text option for quick contact

The business description used throughout the site focuses on:
- Excel automation
- dashboards and reporting
- workflow improvements
- data cleanup and analysis
- business process optimization
- AI-assisted automation where appropriate

Bookings / Scheduling Setup History
-----------------------------------
The scheduling flow was one of the main workstreams.

The user wanted:
- a public booking page
- no Google Workspace dependency
- low-cost setup
- a simple discovery-call flow

Microsoft Bookings was chosen because it fits Microsoft 365 Business Standard and supports a public scheduling link.

Final booking flow:
- Book a Discovery Call button opens the Microsoft Bookings page
- public booking link is used on the website
- the service is set for a 30-minute meeting
- the user’s side-business hours were narrowed to a small evening window

The booking availability troubleshooting involved:
- checking staff availability
- checking service-specific scheduling policy
- checking default scheduling policy
- checking minimum lead time
- ensuring the public page uses the right staff assignment rules
- testing the page for time slots that should or should not appear

The user observed that Microsoft Bookings can show times that do not match the main “work hours” unless the service-level scheduling policy is aligned. The workstream concluded that Bookings has multiple layers:
- staff hours
- service availability
- general booking page availability
- calendar busy/free state

Business hours eventually used for booking:
- Monday-Friday 3:00 PM to 6:00 PM

Key Lessons from the Build
--------------------------
- Separate the bio from the homepage when the homepage needs to convert visitors
- Use folder-based URLs for static site compatibility
- Keep booking and text contact paths simple
- Microsoft Bookings can require service-level and staff-level alignment, not just a general work-hours setting
- A business site feels more trustworthy when it shows concrete example builds and practical case studies
- Keeping the repo Cloudflare-ready does not mean Cloudflare has to be used immediately
